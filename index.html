Welcome to the World of Calligraphy

Calligraphy is the art of writing beautiful and elegant letters by hand. It is a form of artistic expression that has been practiced for centuries in cultures around the world. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced calligrapher, there's always something new to learn and explore in the world of calligraphy.

Brush Pen Calligraphy

Brush pen calligraphy only requires two supplies (a brush pen and paper), and it's a fun and approachable way to get started with any type of calligraphy.

Traditional Pointed Pen Calligraphy

Traditional pointed pen calligraphy styles like Spencerian and Copperplate have been around for years, and you create them with particular strokes and formations.

Forms and Styles

Calligraphy encompasses a wide variety of forms and styles, each with their own unique characteristics and history. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Uncial
  • Carolingian
  • Blackletter
  • Copperplate
  • Spencerian
  • Italic
  • Fraktur
  • Gothic

Letters and Fonts

Calligraphy involves creating beautiful letters and typefaces, often with a focus on both form and function. Here are some of the most popular letters and fonts:

  • Capitalis Monumentalis: A Roman capital letter that was used for inscriptions and monuments.
  • Fraktur: A blackletter font used in German-speaking countries from the 16th century until the mid-20th century.
  • Gothic: A style of lettering that emerged in the 12th century and was commonly used in Europe until the 15th century.
  • Copperplate: A script font that was popular in the 18th century and is often used for wedding invitations and formal events.
